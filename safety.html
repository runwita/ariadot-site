Your email, kept safe

Your email is yours. We treat it that way.

Ariadot reads some of the most personal things you own, so you deserve to know exactly how we handle it. Six promises, in plain words, and one honest limit we won't hide from you.

i.

We never see your password.

You sign in through Google or Apple, the same way you sign in to anything else. Your password never leaves them. We can't see it, type it, or store it.

ii.

We can only read. Never send, reply, or delete.

Google only lets us see what's already in your inbox. We have no way to send mail, reply to anyone, or move a thing to the bin. Not by accident, not on purpose. That limit is set by Google's read-only permission, not just promised by us.

iii.

Names and numbers get swapped before any AI reads them.

Before we ask an AI to help write your note, we change every real name, phone number, address and dollar amount into a believable fake one. The AI reasons about a stranger's details, never yours.

We swap the real ones back at our end, just before the note reaches you, using a map that's sealed with a key unique to you and never sent to any model. What we don't touch are dates and times, because a note that reasons about the wrong day is worse than useless.

iv.

The AI doesn't keep what it sees, or train on it.

We mostly use Claude, made by Anthropic, with a couple of smaller models for the quick sorting and screening. Each one only ever sees the swapped, fake version, and none of them keep it or learn from it.

v.

The raw email doesn't hang around.

Once Ariadot has read a message and written your note, the original is deleted within a day. Most go within minutes; a daily sweep makes sure nothing older than that remains. Your email still lives safely in Gmail, where it always has, so there's no reason for us to keep a copy.

What we do keep is the useful part: your loops, your handbook, and a quiet profile of your routines. You can see all of it in the app.

vi.

You can delete everything yourself.

Settings → Delete account. There's a 7-day window in case you change your mind, then it's gone for good, and your Google connection is switched off at Google's end. No "we'll keep it just in case."

Common worries

The questions you'd ask a friend over coffee.

Honest answers, including the uncomfortable one.

What if I lose my phone?+

Nothing important lives on your phone. Sign in on a new one and your morning notes come back. Your loops and handbook live on our servers, not the phone, so a dropped phone loses nothing.

Do you read my emails yourself?+

No. No one at Ariadot can open your inbox and have a browse, not even our engineers. The only thing that reads your mail is software, doing one quick pass, then moving on.

What if there's a hack?+

We'll be honest here, because this is the one thing we can't promise away. To write your note, our software reads your email in readable form, and we hold the keys to do that, so we are not a vault that only you can open.

What protects you is narrower and real: the only copy that ever leaves our edge to an AI is the swapped, fake-name version; the raw email is gone within a day; your access tokens are sealed with a key unique to you; and everything is encrypted in transit and at rest.

What if you go out of business?+

We'd give you 30 days' notice by email and let you keep what you'd like, then wipe everything. You'll never wake up wondering where your data went.

What if the morning note is wrong?+

Tell Aria what was off and the next one learns from it. And if we ever can't write one because something broke, we say so plainly. No fake notes, no pretending.

Will my whole family be on it?+

Not yet. Right now it's for one person, the one who carries the household mental load. You can already share your handbook with a partner, so the household details live in one place. Full household sharing is something we want to do well, not quickly.
