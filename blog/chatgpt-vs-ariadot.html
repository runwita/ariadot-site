ChatGPT vs Ariadot

"ChatGPT can already do this. Why would I use your app?"

It's the fair question. ChatGPT now has connectors and scheduled tasks, so it can read a connected inbox and run a prompt on a timer. The honest answer isn't that it can't. It's that you have to tell it to, every step of the way, and the whole point of Ariadot is the things you'd never think to ask about.

Comparison · 30 May 2026 · 5 min read

If you're handy with AI, you've probably already wired something up: a ChatGPT scheduled task that "checks my inbox each morning and lists what needs a reply." It works. So does a custom GPT with the Gmail connector. The capability overlap with an email assistant is real, and pretending otherwise would be silly.

But there's a difference between a tool that can do a job when instructed and a product that does that one job, on its own, every day, without you in the loop. That gap is the entire reason Ariadot exists.

A scheduled task runs the prompt you wrote. Ariadot finds the thing you didn't write a prompt for.

The five real differences

1. Proactive, not prompted

A ChatGPT scheduled task executes your instructions. That means you have to know, in advance, what to watch for: "remind me about replies," "flag invoices," "tell me about renewals." But the thing that actually bites you is the one you didn't anticipate, the school form due in three weeks, the parking fine with a quiet deadline, the reply you promised and forgot. Ariadot reads the whole inbox and surfaces what matters without a rule for it. You can't forget to write a prompt for a category you didn't see coming.

2. One narrow job, done the same way every day

A general assistant pointed at your inbox does an open-ended task, and open-ended tasks drift, different emphasis, different misses, different format depending on the day and the wording. Ariadot does exactly one thing: read your email and calendar, track the open loops, and send a short brief twice a day. Narrow and boring is a feature when the job is "never let something slip."

3. Your details never reach a general model unmasked

Connecting a general assistant to your inbox sends your mail (names, addresses, account numbers) to a general-purpose model. Ariadot masks personal details before any cloud model sees the text, and never uses your data to train anything. For something reading every email you get, that posture isn't a nice-to-have.

4. It comes to you

With ChatGPT you open the app, wait for the task, or go re-establish context in a chat. Ariadot pushes one brief to your phone, morning and evening, written in a voice that already knows your week. There's nothing to open and nothing to re-explain.

5. Reliability over capability

ChatGPT is vastly more capable, it'll write your code, plan your trip, draft the email. Ariadot can't do any of that, and won't try. What it offers instead is that the narrow thing happens, dependably, in the background, so the deadline buried on page two of a long thread doesn't become tomorrow's problem.

So which should you use?

Both, honestly. Keep ChatGPT for the open-ended work, it's better at it than anything purpose-built. Use Ariadot for the one job a general assistant can technically do but won't reliably do unattended: catching what's about to slip out of your inbox before it costs you. They're not really competitors. One waits for you to ask. The other has already noticed.
