§ 01 · The premise

It's not a task manager. That's the whole point.

A task manager only knows what you remember to type into it, which means the things you forget never make it in. ariadot works the other way around: it reads your inbox and surfaces the loops on its own, with nothing to set up, no list to build, no reminder to write. It reports. You close.

Loop type · 01

Bleeding

Promises past their date. A confirmation you said you'd send last week. A bill that crossed into overdue. These come first in every brief.

Loop type · 02

Due soon

Anything attached to a real date. A renewal in two weeks, an invoice on Thursday, a friend's birthday this weekend. Surfaced with enough runway to actually do something.

Loop type · 03

Information

Soft loops. Context worth carrying. "You told her you'd send the link by Friday." Nothing on fire, just a string you don't want to drop.

And it quietly keeps the things that aren't loops at all, just worth remembering. The insurance policy number. Your anniversary. The membership ID buried in a welcome email. The subscription you meant to cancel three months ago. Not password-manager secrets, just the small important details you'd never think to file, until the one moment you need them.

§ 02 · How it works

Connect your email. That's the whole setup.

No tagging, no rules, no folders to maintain. ariadot reads your inbox, does the work, and writes you a brief. Twice a day, in plain prose.

i.

15 seconds

Plug in your email.

Read-only Google OAuth for Gmail and Calendar. We never see your password. No folders to make, no rules to write. The app starts working the moment the connection lands.

ii.

Background, ongoing

We read, mask, and reason.

Messages are pulled to our processing edge on Cloudflare, masked with realistic, natural-looking stand-ins, and reasoned over by Claude. Drafts get a small model. Hard calls get a bigger one.

iii.

Twice a day

The morning brief sets the shape of your day. The evening brief winds it down. Both are read only, on purpose. When you want to close, snooze, or hand something off, that lives on the Loops page.

§ 03 · Privacy architecture

We treat your inbox the way it deserves to be treated.

Email is one of the most personal datasets you own. We know that. The privacy model below was designed before the product was, and the product was built around it.

Ariadot reads your email so you don't have to. That means we are temporarily handling some of the most sensitive content you own: your relationships, your finances, your medical correspondence, your travel, your conversations with your family. We hold that responsibility seriously. Everything is encrypted at rest. Everything that resembles PII is masked before any model sees it. And the masks we send are not robotic placeholders, they are realistic, plausible stand-ins drawn from a synthetic corpus we built specifically for this.

The pipeline below describes every place your data exists, from inbox to brief. Three colors, three locations. Read it left to right.

MASKING · WORKED EXAMPLE

What the model actually sees.

Here is the same line of email in both forms. The left card is what arrived in your inbox. The right card is what we send Claude. Identifiers are swapped for realistic stand-ins, not placeholders, because natural-looking text leads to better reasoning than alien tokens, and the stand-ins are uncorrelated with the real values.

What arrived From: priya.menon@gmail.com. Hey, can you forward the invoice from Sigma Studios for the $1,840 shoot? Trying to close out the May books before Friday. My mobile is +61 412 558 904 if easier.
What Claude sees From: helena.dorff@gmail.com. Hey, can you forward the invoice from Bramley House for the $2,610 shoot? Trying to close out the May books before Friday. My mobile is +61 478 211 663 if easier.

Why this works. Every masked field is drawn from a synthetic dataset of names, addresses, amounts, phone numbers, IDs, and free-text fragments. The stand-ins are realistic and statistically diverse, but uncorrelated with anything in your real life. Even if the model output were leaked, it would describe a coherent stranger, not you. The mapping back to your real values is held only on our edge, sealed with a per-user key, and never reaches any model. Notice what didn't change: "May" and "Friday" stay exactly as written. Dates, times, and deadlines are never shifted, because a brief that reasons about the wrong day is worse than useless.

The masks are stable across sessions. Continuity matters. If "Mary" gets masked as "Jackson" today, she will still be "Jackson" in tomorrow's brief and next month's brief. That stability is what makes reasoning over time possible: the model sees a consistent cast of characters across your week, even though it never sees the real ones.

PROMISES

Six things we will not do, in plain language

i.

No training on your data, ever.

Not for us, not for the model providers. We do not train anything on your email, and our API access to Anthropic, OpenAI, and Cloudflare is under terms that do not use API traffic for training. We have no model of our own to train.

ii.

No ads, no data brokers.

ariadot is paid for by ariadot subscribers. We have no advertising business, no analytics business, and no "data partner" of any kind.

iii.

No human review of your briefs.

Engineers and support staff cannot read your briefs or your email. There is no internal tool that surfaces them. When you write to support, you choose what to share, and we mask what you send before it lands in a ticket.

iv.

No silent expansion of scope.

Ariadot's access is read-only Gmail and Calendar, and that is all we ask for. If we ever need a new permission or change how data is handled, you will be asked to approve it and told plainly what changed, in the app and in this policy. We will not change the rules quietly.

v.

No retention beyond what's necessary.

The raw email and calendar we fetch is deleted once Ariadot has read it and written your brief, within 24 hours, not kept around. What stays is the useful output: your loops, your handbook, and the profile Aria builds, all visible to you in the app and held until you delete your account. The model providers retain nothing of what we send.

vi.

No dark patterns on the way out.

One tap to delete, one tap to revoke our email access. No "are you sure" gauntlet, no dark patterns. Deletion runs with a 7-day grace window, so a mistaken tap is recoverable by signing back in; after 7 days it is permanent and irreversible, and your Google access is revoked upstream. When you leave, you leave clean.

§ 06 · Security

The boring, important stuff.

No badges we haven't earned. Just what's actually true about how Ariadot is built today.

Encryption

  • Encrypted in transit at every hop: app to our edge, edge to Gmail, edge to every model provider.
  • Encrypted at rest.
  • Your Google access tokens are additionally sealed with a per-user key, so a partial exposure doesn't unlock them.

Where it runs

  • All processing on Cloudflare Workers, with state isolated per user.
  • Only masked content is ever sent to a model.
  • Raw email and calendar is deleted within 24 hours of producing your brief.

Untrusted input

  • Your email is untrusted by default. Every message is screened for prompt-injection before any reasoning, and content is fenced off from instructions.
  • A credential guard drops anything resembling a password, OTP, or secret before storage.
  • Semgrep runs in CI on every change.

Access & sign-in

  • Sign in with Apple or Google. No passwords for us to lose.
  • Gmail and Calendar are read-only: we cannot send, delete, or change anything.
  • Access to our own systems is protected with multi-factor authentication.

Sub-processors

  • Named in our privacy policy: Cloudflare, Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and TickTick (only if you connect it).
  • No advertisers, no data brokers, no analytics resold.
  • Each receives only the minimum needed, masked where it leaves our edge.

What we don't claim

  • We're a small team in private beta. We don't yet hold SOC 2 or a published audit, and we won't pretend otherwise.
  • We process your email in readable form to do the work, and hold the keys to do it. We are not zero-knowledge, and we don't claim to be.
  • What we promise is on this page, and it's true today.
§ 07 · Asked & answered

The questions we get most.

Mostly about the models and the masking. Reasonable questions, honest answers.

Which models do you use, and why those?+

A small set, each picked for a job. Anthropic's Claude Sonnet does the delicate work, writing the brief in your voice and judging the shape of a day; Claude Haiku handles lighter Aria tasks. OpenAI's smallest model does fast first-pass classification, deciding what's even worth a closer look. And Llama, running on Cloudflare Workers AI, does the privacy-sensitive screening, including the credential guard that catches passwords and secrets.

Every one of them only ever receives masked input, and none of them retains or trains on it. We're also exploring running a model (Kimi) locally on Cloudflare Workers, so that more of the reasoning can happen without your data leaving our own edge at all.

Why mask with realistic names instead of «person_3» placeholders?+

Two reasons. First, models reason better over natural text. Placeholders like «person_3» degrade the quality of judgement and write awkward briefs. Second, the masks are drawn from a 50,000-row synthetic dataset that is statistically diverse and uncorrelated with anything real. So if model output were ever leaked, it would describe a coherent stranger, not you.

This is also why we never send obvious markers like [REDACTED] in their place. The model should not even know it is reasoning over masked data; that is the whole point.

So the AI models never see my real email content?+

Correct. Every API call to a model contains masked text only: names, emails, addresses, phone numbers, account references, amounts, and any free text that resembles PII have all been swapped for stand-ins before the call is built. The mapping between real and masked values is held only on our edge, sealed with a per-user key, and never sent to any model.

The mapping is also stable per user: once Mary becomes Jackson, she stays Jackson across every future brief. That continuity is what makes reasoning over your week possible, while still keeping the real names off the wire.

The strongest argument for this is not trust, it is that we built the system so that "trust us" is not load bearing.

What about passwords, OTPs, or secrets in my email?+

A credential guard runs over every message before any reasoning. If something looks like a password, one-time code, API key, or recovery phrase, the message is dropped, not stored and not sent to a model. The whole point of Ariadot is the loops and dates, never your secrets.

Why process on Cloudflare instead of on device?+

Because the input to Ariadot is your email, and email lives on a server, not on your phone. A fully on-device design would mean constantly pulling your whole inbox onto the device, which is heavy, battery-hungry, and worse for privacy than people assume.

Cloudflare Workers let us do the work in an encrypted environment with strict per-user isolation, then delete the raw material once your brief is written. The app is a thin client: it shows you the brief, the loops, and your handbook, and holds nothing it doesn't need to.

What about prompt injection? My email is full of untrusted content.+

Treated as a first-class threat. Every message is scanned for prompt-injection patterns before reasoning, and untrusted content is wrapped in clearly delineated boundaries so the model cannot mistake email body for instructions. Output is also constrained to a strict schema so the model cannot ask the system to do anything outside its job. We track the OWASP LLM Top 10 as our working list, and run Semgrep in CI to catch design-time vulnerabilities on every PR.

Where is the brief read, and can I act from it?+

The brief is intentionally read only. It exists to give you a clear, calm summary, not to lure you into clicking. To close a loop, snooze it, or hand it off, you move to the Loops page. That separation keeps the brief honest and keeps you in control of what gets actioned.

I'm not in the EU. Do I still get GDPR-style rights?+

Yes. We apply GDPR-equivalent rights to every ariadot user regardless of jurisdiction: access, correction, portability, erasure, and the right to know who we share with. Right of erasure is a single button. See §05 above.

How long do you keep my email?+

The raw email and calendar we fetch is deleted within 24 hours of writing your brief. It's re-fetchable from Google if ever needed, so there's no reason to hold it. What stays is the useful output, your loops, handbook, and the profile Aria builds, and that's kept until you delete your account. Delete runs with a 7-day grace window, then it's permanent and your Google access is revoked.

Can anyone at Ariadot read my email or my briefs?+

No. There is no internal tool that surfaces your briefs or your inbox, and we don't review them. When you contact support, you choose what to share. We process your email content to do the work and we hold the keys required to do that, so we're honest that we are not a zero-knowledge service. What we won't do is read your mail for any reason other than running the product for you.

Is everything encrypted?+

Yes, in transit at every hop, from the app to our edge, from our edge to Gmail, and from our edge to each model provider, and at rest. Your Google access tokens get an extra layer: they're sealed with a key unique to your account, so even a partial data exposure doesn't hand someone your inbox.

Can I see everything Ariadot knows about me?+

Yes, and that's deliberate. Everything Aria keeps is visible in the app: your loops, your handbook, the profile Aria has formed of your routines, and an Activity log showing what it decided about each email it read. Nothing is hidden in a backend you can't see.

How does ariadot make money?+

It will be a subscription, plain and simple, paid for by the people who use it. It's free during the private beta. There is no free tier funded by data, no ads, and no "data partner" of any kind. If we ever change the business model, you'll hear about it here first.

What platforms is it on?+

iOS and Android, in private beta now. macOS and Windows are not on the near roadmap; the briefs are designed to live in your pocket.
